What is Cognitive Science?

An overall goal is to better understand the interaction between people and their environment, in order to contribute to the development of systems, artifacts and methods that work well for people. Cognitive Science explores the thinking from multiple scientific perspectives. The phenomena of perception, behavior, memory, learning, communication, concept formation, decision making, and human interaction with and in complex environments is central. These phenomena are studied by combinations of knowledge and methods from psychology, computer science, neuroscience, biology, linguistics, philosophy, anthropology, education, and informatics. The multifaceted approach based on: artificial systems such as robots and virtual agents; biological systems, particularly, humans and other animals; how knowledge creatures evolve over time: an evolutionary and individually, and how the structures of cognitive processes vary under different circumstances.
